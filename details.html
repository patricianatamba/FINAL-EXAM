Shea Butter

Local Name: Muzigo gw'ebititi

Safety Status: Safe

Shea butter is a fat extracted from the nut of the African shea tree. It is widely used in cosmetics as a moisturizer, salve or lotion. It absorbs rapidly into the skin without leaving a greasy residue and is known for its incredible healing properties.

Benefits

  • Deeply moisturizes dry skin
  • Reduces skin inflammation
  • Provides anti-aging properties
  • Restores skin elasticity

Potential Side Effects

  • Extremely rare allergic reactions
  • Can be comedogenic for some very acne-prone skin types if used in heavy amounts

Hydroquinone

Local Name: Kola-bbala

Safety Status: Avoid

Hydroquinone is a chemical skin-lightening agent used to bleach the skin. It decreases the formation of melanin in the skin. While sometimes prescribed medically for severe hyperpigmentation, its widespread unregulated use in cosmetic bleaching creams is highly dangerous and illegal in many countries including Uganda.

Why to Avoid

  • Causes severe skin thinning
  • Can result in Ochronosis (blue-black discoloration of the skin)
  • Increases risk of skin cancer due to UV sensitivity
  • Damages the skin's natural protective barrier

Safe Alternatives

  • Vitamin C (Vitayimini C)
  • Turmeric (Ebizigo by'ekinzaali)
  • Niacinamide
  • Licorice Root Extract
